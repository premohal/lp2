# 🎯 How to Explain This Code to External

When external asks, don’t explain every line immediately.
Explain in this flow 👇

---

# 🔴 Introduction

> “This program solves the N-Queens Problem using CSP (Constraint Satisfaction Problem) and Backtracking.”

---

# 🔴 Explain N-Queens Problem

Say:

> “The goal is to place N queens on chessboard such that no two queens attack each other.”

Then explain:

Queens can attack:

* Row
* Column
* Diagonal

---

# 🔴 Show Example

For N = 4

```text id="m7x9d2"
. Q . .
. . . Q
Q . . .
. . Q .
```

Then say:

> “Here no queens are attacking each other.”

---

# 🔴 Explain Chessboard

Say:

> “The board is stored using 2D list.”

Example:

```python id="t9f0x1"
board = [[0 for i in range(N)] for j in range(N)]
```

Then say:

> “0 means empty cell and 1 means queen placed.”

---

# 🔴 Explain Backtracking

Say confidently:

> “Backtracking means trying one solution, and if it fails, going back and trying another solution.”

Flow:

```text id="f5n1pq"
Place Queen
   ↓
Check Safe Position
   ↓
Move Next Column
   ↓
If Failed → Backtrack
```

---

# 🔴 Explain Important Arrays

---

## left_row

> “Checks whether queen already exists in same row.”

---

## lower_diagonal

> “Checks lower diagonal safety.”

Formula:

row+col

---

## upper_diagonal

> “Checks upper diagonal safety.”

Formula:

N-1+col-row

---

# 🔴 Explain Important Functions

---

## print_board()

> “This function prints final chessboard.”

Then explain:

* Q → Queen
* . → Empty space

---

## solve(col)

> “This is recursive backtracking function.”

Explain flow:

```text id="x7b2rm"
1. Start from first column
2. Try placing queen in every row
3. Check row and diagonal safety
4. Place queen if safe
5. Recursively solve next column
6. If failed, remove queen
7. Try another position
```

---

# 🔴 Explain Base Condition

Say:

```python id="n2v4kt"
if col == N:
    return True
```

Then explain:

> “If all queens are placed successfully, solution is found.”

---

# 🔴 Explain Backtracking Step

Show this:

```python id="q8s1de"
board[row][col]=0
```

Then say:

> “If solution fails, queen is removed and another position is tried.”

---

# 🎯 VERY IMPORTANT VIVA QUESTIONS

---

## ❓ What is N-Queens Problem?

> “Placing N queens such that no queens attack each other.”

---

## ❓ What is CSP?

> “Constraint Satisfaction Problem.”

---

## ❓ What constraints are checked?

> “Row and diagonals.”

---

## ❓ Why column check is not needed?

> “Because only one queen is placed per column.”

---

## ❓ What is Backtracking?

> “Trying solution recursively and undoing if it fails.”

---

## ❓ Why recursion is used?

> “To place queens column by column.”

---

## ❓ What does left_row store?

> “It stores whether row already has queen.”

---

## ❓ What is lower diagonal formula?

row+col

---

## ❓ What is upper diagonal formula?

N-1+col-row

---

## ❓ Time Complexity?

> “Approximately O(N!).”

---

## ❓ Space Complexity?

> “O(N²) for chessboard.”

---

# 🎯 MOST IMPORTANT THING

External mostly checks:
